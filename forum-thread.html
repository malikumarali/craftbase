Best materials for earthquake-resistant homes in Pakistan?

General 👤 Ahmed K. 📅 Feb 12, 2026 ▲ 28 upvotes 12 replies

We are planning to build a 2-storey home in a seismic zone 3 area near Muzaffarabad. The architect has recommended reinforced concrete frame (RCC) but our contractor is suggesting we could save money by using load-bearing brickwork for the upper floor.

I am worried because of 2005 earthquake memory. What materials and structural approaches should we absolutely insist on with the contractor? Are there specific PEC guidelines for this?

RCC frame is absolutely the right call for Zone 3. Do NOT let the contractor cut corners here. The 2005 earthquake was devastating specifically because of load-bearing masonry failures. Get a structural engineer to sign off on the design — this is mandatory under PEC regulations for Zone 3.

Great question. As a PEC-registered firm, here is what we recommend for Zone 3:

1. RCC Frame only — never load-bearing masonry above ground floor in Zone 3+
2. Grade 60 rebar (not Grade 40) for all columns and beams
3. M25 concrete mix minimum for structural elements
4. Shear walls at key locations per structural engineer design
5. Always get an independent structural engineer — not just rely on the contractor's team

We have delivered 15+ Zone 3 projects. Happy to share our structural checklist via DM.
